There is enough closeness to feel connected, but enough space to feel safe and respected.

Analogy mapping Atoms → individuals
Bond distance → emotional distance
Potential energy → emotional energy of the relationship
Main lesson Two individuals tend to find a unique optimal emotional distance: not too far and not too close.
Important nuance Different people, friendships, couples, and teams can have different “equilibrium distances.” One size does not fit all.
Energy release When two people move from emotional distance toward a better connection, the relationship can feel rewarding, energizing, and warm.
Energy absorption When a friendship breaks or people drift away from their comfortable distance, it can feel effortful, painful, or draining.
Extension to groups Teams also need a healthy balance between closeness and space: enough trust to collaborate, enough autonomy to breathe.
This is an analogy, not a scientific model of psychology. It works best as a visual metaphor for comfort, connection, boundaries, and “chemistry.”
